# Content Standards & Guidelines

This document defines the editorial standards, voice, and formatting rules for all content within the LightSpeed Tour Operator prototype.

## Voice and Tone

The brand voice is **adventurous, authoritative, inspiring, yet accessible**.
- **Adventurous**: Use evocative language that sparks wanderlust (e.g., "Embark," "Discover," "Unforgettable").
- **Authoritative**: Demonstrate expertise in African travel without being academic.
- **Inspiring**: Focus on the *experience* and *feeling* of the destination.
- **Accessible**: Avoid jargon; make luxury travel feel welcoming.

## Formatting Rules

### Headings
- **Sentence Case**: All headings (H1-H6) must use sentence case. Capitalize only the first word and proper nouns.
  - ✅ **Correct**: "Cape Town honeymoon escape"
  - ❌ **Incorrect**: "Cape Town Honeymoon Escape"
  - ✅ **Correct**: "Discover your next adventure"
  - ❌ **Incorrect**: "Discover Your Next Adventure"

### Brand Name Usage
- **Italicization**: The following generic brand terms must be **italicized** whenever they appear in content:
  - *Tour Operator*
  - *Tour Operator Plugin*
  - *Tour Operator Website Solutions*
- **Specific Brand**: "LightSpeed Tours" does not strictly require italics unless specified, but sticking to the generic terms above is preferred for white-label prototyping.

## Date & Time
- Use **ISO 8601** (YYYY-MM-DD) for machine-readable dates.
- Use **Long Form** (e.g., "January 1, 2024") for display text.

## Currency
- Use standard formatting: "$2,999" (not "2999 USD" unless necessary for clarity).
